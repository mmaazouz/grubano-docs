# Menu & AI Scan

Put your menu online by simply photographing your dishes: AI recognizes each recipe, suggests name, description, and allergens, and you just need to validate.

## The essentials

The AI scanner transforms a menu photo into a structured dish card — name, appetizing description, allergens, estimated calories, category — in a few seconds. You verify, adjust the price, and publish. Ideal for accelerating the online launch of a new menu or adding a daily special, without tedious manual entry.

## How it works

Photographing a paper menu to retype it line by line is time-consuming and error-prone. The AI scanner reads the image, identifies each visible dish, and automatically generates a complete card in French: commercial name, two-sentence pitch, ingredient list, standardized allergens (Gluten, Lactose, Egg, Soy…), calorie range, category (Starters, Main courses, Desserts, Drinks), and suggested labels (Vegetarian, Halal, Gluten-free, Spicy). You receive a ready-to-use JSON that the [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/) integrates directly into your menu: you only need to set the price and validate. The recognition runs on a Claude Sonnet 4.5 model trained on restaurant menus — it even spots handwritten dishes or dense layouts.

<Callout type="info">
The AI also suggests variants (Small/Medium/Large size, Cheese/Bacon supplements…) and a preparation note if it detects a special mention on the menu. These options are optional: you activate or remove them according to your actual offering.
</Callout>

## Step by step

<Steps>

### Access the scanner

In the [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/), **Menu** section, click the **To adopt** tab then **Scan a menu** (camera icon). Authorize camera access or select an image already taken.

### Photograph the dish

Frame the dish or menu section describing it. Natural lighting and straight framing improve recognition. Confirm the shot.

### Verify the generated card

The AI returns a structured preview in ~3 seconds: name, description, ingredients, allergens, min–max calories, category, labels. Review each field — the AI is reliable but may confuse a rare or misspelled ingredient.

### Adjust and publish

Edit the name or description if necessary, enter the sale price (the AI doesn’t read amounts to avoid currency errors), check **Available**, then **Save**. The dish appears immediately on your public menu in the detected category.

</Steps>

## Concrete example

You’re opening a burger counter and want to put ten signature recipes online. Rather than filling out ten forms by hand, you photograph your printed menu section by section. ## Best practices

- **Photograph dish by dish** if your menu is dense: the AI processes a single recipe better than a full page with fifteen starters.
- **Indirect lighting**: avoid reflections on a laminated menu that obscure the text.
- **Verify allergens**: the AI bases itself on visible ingredients; a hidden condiment (mustard in a vinaigrette) may escape it. Manually add any missing allergen.
- **Set the price yourself**: the AI never reads amounts to avoid confusion between VAT-inclusive price, currency, or promotion. It’s the only mandatory field to enter after scanning.

<Callout type="warning">
The AI scanner consumes API credits per analyzed photo. A reasonable limit (a few dozen scans per day) applies by default to preserve shared resources. If you reach the limit, try again in a few hours or contact support for an expanded quota.
</Callout>

## Common errors

**"The AI doesn’t recognize my handwritten dish."** — The handwriting must remain legible; very stylized calligraphy or text that’s too small reduces accuracy. Take the photo closer or manually retype the name, then let the AI generate the description from that name.

**"The suggested calories seem wrong."** — The AI gives a broad range (e.g., 650–750 kcal) based on standard portions for the dish type. Adjust the value if you know the exact weight of your portions or leave blank: calorie display is optional on the customer side.

**"The dish appears in the wrong category."** — Manually change the category (Starters, Main courses, Desserts, Drinks) before publishing. The AI guesses from the name and ingredients, but a "Tuna Carpaccio" may hesitate between Starters and Main courses depending on context.

## Tips & advice

- **Reuse cards**: once scanned, a recipe remains editable in your menu. Change the price or availability without rescanning.
- **Scan daily specials**: photograph the lunch board, validate the three suggestions, publish in two minutes. In the evening, deactivate them with one click in [Stock management](/en/guides/stocks/) or the dashboard.
- **Enrich the description**: the AI produces two generic sentences; add a signature detail ("meat aged 21 days," "homemade dough") to stand out.
