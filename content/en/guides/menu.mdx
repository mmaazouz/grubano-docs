## The principle (and the why)

Manually retyping a 30-dish menu takes time — and allergen mistakes can be costly. The AI scanner reads your paper menu (or a PDF), recognizes each dish and pre-fills the fields: all you have to do is check and adjust the price. The dishes are added to your brand (the owning Brand) and appear immediately in the [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/) — available to your customers as soon as they are published.

The scan uses a vision model trained on thousands of restaurant menus. It identifies common allergens (gluten, lactose, egg, tree nuts…), estimates calories and classifies each dish (starter, main, dessert, drink). You stay in control: no data is published without your approval.

## Digitize your menu

<Steps>

### Access the scanner

From `/menu`, click the **Menu & AI Scan** tab. You will see the "Scan a dish" button.

### Photograph the dish

Take a sharp photo of the menu line (or upload an image from your gallery). Accepted formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF. The AI supports printed and handwritten menus as well as PDF screenshots.

### Review the suggestions

The AI returns a JSON object with `name`, `description`, `ingredients`, `allergens` (filtered list: Gluten, Lactose, Egg, Soy, Peanut, Tree nuts, Fish, Crustaceans, Molluscs, Celery, Mustard, Sesame, Sulphites, Lupin), `calories_min`/`calories_max`, `category` (Starters, Mains, Desserts, Drinks) and `suggested_labels` (Veggie, Halal, Gluten-free, Spicy). Correct any approximations.

### Set the price and publish

The scan does not guess prices — enter the tax-inclusive amount. Click "Add to menu": the dish is created in your brand (field `brandId`) with `available: true` by default. It appears at once on your restaurant page.

</Steps>

## Improve recognition

<Callout type="default">
- **Well-framed photo**: isolate one dish at a time — if the menu has 10, scan them one by one. Recognition is more reliable on a single line.
- **Light and contrast**: avoid glare and cast shadows. A light background with black text gives the best results.
- **Always check the allergens**: the AI extracts them from the visible description, but if an ingredient isn’t mentioned on the menu, it can’t guess it. Fill it in manually if needed.
- **Consistent categories**: the `category` field is trimmed server-side to avoid duplicates ("Drinks" vs "  Drinks  "). Keep the same labels to group your dishes cleanly.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Approving without rereading**: the AI makes suggestions, not certainties. A missed allergen or a misread price can trigger a customer complaint.
- **Scanning a whole menu**: sending a photo of several dishes at once produces a single JSON with mixed-up data. The result: you will have to fix everything by hand.
- **Forgetting the final photos**: the scan analyzes the image of the menu but does not store it as the dish photo. Add a real food photo from the edit form to entice your customers.
</Callout>

## Save time with batch scanning

<Callout type="info">
If you have 20 dishes to digitize, prepare all your photos in advance (one per dish, well framed). Scan them one after another: the interface keeps the focus on the scanner, so you just load the next image, check, adjust the price and approve. In 10 minutes, your menu is complete.
</Callout>

## Adopt creator recipes

Your menu isn’t limited to your own dishes: on the Menu screen, the **To adopt** tab opens the catalog of recipes published by [creators](/en/guides/creators/). Each card presents the dish, its description and its terms; in one click, the recipe joins your menu like any other dish — you cook it under your own banner, you set the selling price, and the creator earns 2 % on every sale of the adopted dish.

Some recipes are exclusive by city: if another restaurant near you has already adopted one, join the waitlist from the same tab. When a spot opens up, you receive a time-limited offer that you accept or decline in one click — if you let it pass, it is offered to the next restaurant on the list.
