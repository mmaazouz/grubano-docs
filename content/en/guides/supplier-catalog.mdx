## The principle (and the why)

Grubano connects suppliers of provisions with local restaurants. Unlike a paper catalog or an email purchase order, your online offering is **always up to date**: when you change a price or mark a product unavailable, every restaurant sees it immediately.

You define your **commercial terms** just once (delivery zones, minimum order, preparation time, payment terms): restaurants know in advance whether they can order from you, and you only receive requests that fit your operation. Order settlement will go through a secure payment provider — this building block is coming **soon**; in the meantime, you arrange payment directly with your customers.

## Publish your catalog and receive your first orders

<Steps>

### Build your catalog

Go to your supplier workspace. Add a product: name, description (optional), category (meat, dairy, grocery…), **unit price in euros** (stored in cents on the system side to avoid rounding), unit (kg, L, piece, case…), packaging ("case of 6", "5 kg bag"…). Tick **Available**; untick if out of stock. You can flag allergens (optional, INCO standard). Repeat for each item.

### Define your commercial offer

Go to **Profile**. Fill in your **delivery zones** (cities or postal areas served), the **minimum order** (amount in euros), the **preparation time** (in days) and your **payment terms** (free text: bank transfer, cheque, payment window…). These settings filter restaurants: outside the zone or below the threshold, they won't see the "Order" button.

### Receive an order

A restaurant browses your catalog, adds line items to its cart and checks out. You receive the order in the **Orders** tab: you see the customer's name, their city, the lines ordered (product, quantity, unit price captured at the time of the order), the total in cents, the requested date and any notes.

### Prepare and deliver

Prepare the order following your usual process. Arrange delivery or pickup with the restaurant (address and contact shown in the order details). For now, payment is settled directly between you — the secure payment gateway is coming **soon**.

</Steps>

## Tips for an effective catalog

<Callout type="default">
- **Clear descriptions**: mention the origin, the label (organic, PDO…) or the format ("500 g tray") to remove any ambiguity.
- **Consistent categories**: group your products by category (fresh, meat, fish, drinks…) to make browsing easier.
- **Up-to-date stock**: untick **Available** as soon as a product is out of stock — the restaurant won't be able to add it to the cart and you avoid cancellations.
- **Realistic zones**: list only the areas you actually serve; a minimum order matched to your shipping costs reduces abandoned carts.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Forgetting the cents storage**: you enter prices in **euros** in the form ("12,50" for 12,50 €), but the system stores them in cents (1 250) to guarantee precision — don't multiply by 100 manually.
- **Vague zones**: "Paris region" is too vague; prefer "Paris 75001–75020, 92, 93, 94" or a precise list of cities.
- **Frozen catalog**: if you never update your prices or availability, restaurants will order products that no longer exist or at outdated prices.
</Callout>
