## The principle (and the why)

The supplier marketplace is a **professional** sourcing channel, separate from your private supplier directory and from your customers' orders. It shows only **active** suppliers with a verified catalog, and only their **available** items. Each card states the city, the product families, the number of products, the delivery lead time and the minimum order; detailed delivery zones appear when you open the supplier's profile.

At confirmation, your browser sends **no amount**: the server recalculates everything from the catalog prices and locks each line (name, unit, price in cents) into an immutable snapshot. Placing an order triggers no online payment, and this channel activates no loyalty points, no promotions and no referral — it is a flow separate from the customer journey.

## Placing your first supplier order

<Steps>

### Open the marketplace

From your [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/), open the **supplier marketplace**. Each card shows the supplier's name, city, product families, number of products, delivery lead time and — if they have set one — their minimum order.

### Search by product

The search field narrows the list to suppliers offering an **available item** matching your query ("mozzarella", "flour"…). Matching items appear directly on the relevant supplier's card, so you don't have to open every catalog.

### Browse the catalog

Open a supplier's profile to view their full catalog. A **"Delivers to your area"** badge appears when your restaurant's city matches the delivery zones declared by the supplier. Temporarily unavailable items are greyed out and cannot be added to the cart.

### Fill your cart

Adjust quantities with the + / − buttons, filter by search or by product family. A bar at the bottom of the screen tracks your **progress toward the minimum order** and shows the amount still to reach: the "View cart" button only activates once the cart holds at least one item **and** reaches the minimum. The cart is kept in your browser, supplier by supplier — you can come back later without losing anything.

### Confirm the order

The cart screen reloads prices **from the server** (those are the ones that count) and recaps your lines. Choose a **requested delivery date** from five consecutive days — the first suggestion starts from today plus the supplier's preparation lead time — and add a **note** if needed (time slot, access, instructions… 1,000 characters maximum). On confirmation, the order is created with the **"sent"** status, with no online payment.

### Track, cancel or reorder

The **Orders** page lists your supplier orders with tabs by status (all, sent, confirmed, in preparation, delivered) and their counters. Open an order to see its timeline, its lines and its total. While it is "sent", you can **cancel** it; "Reorder" reloads the same lines into your cart; "Contact" opens the supplier's phone or email directly.

</Steps>

Imagine a fruit-and-vegetable supplier with a minimum order of 120 € and a preparation lead time of 2 days. You fill your cart:

## Order without friction

<Callout type="default">
- **Check the zones and the minimum before filling the cart**: the supplier card shows them right away, and the "Delivers to your area" badge confirms the match with your restaurant's city.
- **Use the order note** for your receiving instructions (time slot, access, dock…): it is sent to the supplier with the order.
- **Use "Reorder"** for your recurring purchases: the previous order's lines are reloaded into the cart, and both prices and availability are rechecked at the new confirmation.
- **Group your needs** with a single supplier to reach the minimum in one order, and reconcile your deliveries with your [stock management](/en/guides/stocks/) to keep an up-to-date journal.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Confusing cart and order**: the cart lives in your browser; until you confirm, nothing is sent to the supplier — and you won't find it on another device.
- **Waiting to cancel**: cancellation is only possible while the order is "sent"; as soon as the supplier confirms, use the "Contact" button to sort things out directly.
- **Looking for a timestamp at every step**: only the "Order sent" step carries a real time; the following steps show the status reached, and delivery shows your requested date as the expected date.
- **Counting on loyalty points**: supplier orders are a separate professional channel — no points, no promotions and no referral apply.
</Callout>
