# Franchise on Grubano

Grow your brand through a network or operate an existing brand, within the limits set by Grubano.

<FlowDiagram steps={["Apply","Grubano Validation","Adopt a Brand","Operate"]} />

## The Essentials

The franchise allows you to become a **franchisor** — open your brand to other restaurateurs who will operate it in their kitchen — or a **franchisee** — join an existing brand and cook its menu in your establishment. The brand, menu, and standards remain the property of the franchisor; each outlet operates in its own kitchen.

## How It Works

Grubano offers you two paths: if you own a brand that performs well (rating ≥ 4.5 stars, stable volume), you can open it to the network; eventually, you will receive royalties on the revenue of each franchisee (automated royalty calculation and payment are coming soon to the platform). If you are a restaurateur looking for a proven concept, you apply to operate a brand open to franchising — you cook its menu in your kitchen, under your responsibility, and will pay a royalty to the franchisor.

This model addresses two needs: accelerate the growth of a successful brand without investing in new premises, and allow a restaurateur to start with a validated concept rather than creating one from scratch. The Grubano commission remains **10%** per order, identical for all roles (Standard and Pro). The financial terms between franchisor and franchisee — royalty, entry fees — are set by Grubano when the brand is opened.

## Step by Step

### Becoming a Franchisee (Operating a Brand)

<Steps>

### Discover Open Brands

Visit the **Franchise** page to see brands that are recruiting. Each listing shows the cuisine, average monthly revenue, available cities, and signature dishes.

### Apply

Click **"Apply"** and fill out the form: name, email, business registration number, bank details (optional at this stage), whether you have an equipped kitchen, and a motivation message. Your application is recorded with the status `pending`.

### Wait for Validation

Grubano reviews your application. If the application is approved (`approved`), an **outlet** is automatically created and linked to the brand. You receive a confirmation email. If it is rejected (`rejected`), you are notified.

### Operate

Once validated, your outlet appears in your franchisee space. You cook the brand’s menu, receive orders on your dashboard, and track your performance (revenue, number of orders, royalties owed). The brand remains the property of the franchisor; you are the local operator.

</Steps>

### Becoming a Franchisor (Opening Your Brand to the Network)

<Steps>

### Prepare Your Brand

Ensure that your restaurant displays a rating ≥ 4.5 stars and stable order volume (≥ 50 per week). These criteria guarantee that your concept is proven and replicable.

### Open the Brand to Franchising

From your restaurant account, activate the **"Open to Franchising"** option on one of your brands. Grubano sets the terms with you: royalty (percentage of revenue), entry fees, available geographic areas.

### Receive Applications

Interested restaurateurs apply via the public Franchise page. You receive their applications (name, email, business registration, motivation) and decide to approve or reject each application.

### Manage Your Network

Once franchisees are validated, you access a dedicated dashboard: network revenue, number of active outlets, royalties owed, and performance by establishment. You maintain control of the brand (name, menu, standards); each franchisee cooks in their own kitchen.

</Steps>

## Concrete Example

A restaurateur specializing in vegetarian wraps displays a 4.7-star rating and processes 60 orders per week. He opens his brand to franchising in three cities in his region. A second restaurateur, who has an equipped kitchen but is looking for a validated concept, applies and is approved. The franchisee receives the complete menu (recipes, photos, descriptions), cooks the wraps in their own kitchen. The franchisor tracks the network’s performance from their dashboard; automated royalty payment on revenue is coming soon — each amount owed will then be visible month by month.

## Best Practices

- **Franchisors**: only open your brand to franchising if your indicators are solid (rating ≥ 4.5, regular volume). A fragile concept risks disappointing franchisees and harming your reputation.
- **Franchisees**: carefully read the terms (royalty, entry fees, areas) before applying. Ensure that your kitchen is equipped to cook the brand’s menu.
- **Documentation**: keep a record of approved applications and performance by outlet. This will help you identify establishments that perform well and those that need support.
- **Communication**: stay in regular contact with your franchisees (or your franchisor). A network that communicates well is a network that lasts.

## Common Mistakes

<Callout type="warning">
**Caution**: applying to a brand without having an equipped kitchen slows down the process. Grubano verifies that you can cook the menu before validating your application.
</Callout>

**Opening Too Early**: a franchisor who opens their brand before stabilizing their concept (rating < 4.5, irregular volume) exposes their franchisees to a risk of failure. Consolidate your own restaurant first.

**Not Tracking Performance**: a franchisor who never consults their dashboard misses weak signals (declining revenue, inactive outlets). [Managing your franchise network](/en/guides/franchise-operations/) helps you avoid unpleasant surprises.
