## The idea (and the why)

Grubano links the reservation, the card hold and the bill into **a single record per service**: you book a table → the system blocks a deposit if you enable it → when the customer arrives, the bill opens on that same table → payment frees the table. It all starts from the [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/): set up your dining room and hours once, then let Grubano orchestrate time slots, monitor occupancy and detect overruns.

A single screen gives you the full picture: free tables (green), occupied (orange), running late (red), each with its real-time status. Click a reservation to accept, decline, mark arrival or collect the bill.

## Manage a reservation end to end

<Steps>

### Set up your dining room

From the dashboard, in the **Tables** section, add each table (name, number of seats, position on the plan). The plan is used everywhere: reservations, bills, real-time view. Also set your opening hours — any reservation outside those windows gets a warning.

### Receive the request

The customer books online (a time slot suggested based on your hours and the table's capacity). The request lands in your dashboard with the `confirmed` status. You see the name, number of covers, time, and any allergies or notes.

### Confirm or decline

Accept the reservation as is, or cancel it if the time slot doesn't work (the customer receives an automatic email). If you've enabled the card hold (deposit amount + no-show penalty), Grubano blocks the amount on the customer's card at the time of booking — nothing is charged, the bank just freezes the amount.

### Mark the arrival

On the day, when the customer shows up, click **Arrival**. Grubano moves the reservation to the `arrived` status, releases the card hold (the block clears within 5–7 days), and **automatically opens a bill** on that table. The customer can now view the bill by scanning the table's QR code, or you fill it in from your dashboard.

### Collect and free the table

Once the meal is over, present the bill (customer app or [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/)). The customer pays — card, cash or otherwise — and you confirm the payment. The bill moves to `paid`, the table becomes free again, and the cycle is complete.

</Steps>

## A lunch service with a card hold

A customer books a table for 4 at 12:30 PM, with a 20 € hold and a 15 € no-show penalty. Grubano blocks 20 € on their card (nothing is charged). The customer arrives on time → you mark **Arrival**, the hold is released, the bill opens. The meal comes to 68 €, the customer pays by card, you confirm → the table is freed.

**No-show scenario**: the customer doesn't come and doesn't cancel. You mark **No-show** in the dashboard → Grubano automatically captures the 15 € penalty from the hold (the rest, 5 €, is released). The customer receives an email with the amount charged and the dispute procedure.

## Getting the most out of the system

<Callout type="default">

- **Fill in your hours and closures**: Grubano automatically blocks time slots outside your hours or during special closures (you can force a private reservation, but the system alerts you).
- **Enable the card hold for high-demand services** (Friday/Saturday nights, tasting menus): it drastically reduces no-shows with no effort on your part.
- **Mark "Arrival" as soon as the customer sits down**: the bill opens right away, the customer can view it during the meal, and you save time at checkout.
- **Watch the color code**: an orange table (occupied) turning red (overrun) signals it's time to offer the bill — no rushing, just to keep the turnover flowing.

</Callout>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">

- **Forgetting to confirm the arrival**: if you don't mark "Arrival," the bill won't open automatically — the customer can neither view nor pay online.
- **Manually releasing a hold**: Grubano does it for you (arrival → release, no-show → capture) — stepping in directly with the payment provider risks desyncing the status.
- **Not filling in the floor plan**: without a configured table, you can't take reservations — selecting a table is mandatory.
- **Capturing the hold on a normal arrival**: the hold is only captured in case of a no-show; an arrival always releases it (the block clears with no charge).

</Callout>
