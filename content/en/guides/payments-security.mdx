# Payments & security

How Grubano secures payments, protects banking data, and handles refunds and disputes — with full transparency.

<ArticleMeta audience="Restaurants" minutes={4} updated="2026-07-12" />

<Eyebrow icon="insights">Overview</Eyebrow>

## Payment, from order to payout

The customer pays for their order **online, by card**, at the moment they confirm it. The transaction is processed by **Stripe**, our payment provider: it is encrypted and protected by **3D Secure** strong authentication. Grubano collects on behalf of your restaurant, deducts the commission based on the order's **mode** (see the [commission by mode](/en/guides/finances/)), then pays out the net to you automatically. You have **no unpaid orders to chase**: an order only exists once its payment is confirmed.

## Banking data security

**PCI-DSS** compliance (the security standard of the payment card industry) is handled by Stripe. In practice:

- Grubano **never sees or stores card numbers**. When the customer enters their card, the data goes directly to Stripe, without passing through our servers in clear text.
- Cards saved by customers are kept **on Stripe's side, in tokenized form**: Grubano only handles a technical token, never the card number.
- The **partners' bank details** (those used for payouts, including yours) are also managed by the payment provider — never stored by Grubano.

<Callout type="info">
So neither you nor Grubano has any sensitive banking data to protect: it never leaves Stripe's certified infrastructure.
</Callout>

## Refunds

Three principles apply to any refunded order, whatever the reason:

- **The Grubano commission is returned pro rata** to the refunded amount. Grubano keeps nothing on a refunded order — full refund, commission returned in full; partial refund, commission returned in the same proportion.
- **The amount is capped**: it is impossible to refund more than what the customer actually paid.
- **No double refund**: every refunded amount is tracked; a euro already returned cannot be returned a second time.

On the customer's side, the refund appears in their account **within a few business days, depending on their bank** (standard interbank delays).

<Callout type="info">
The self-service refund flow from your dashboard is coming **soon**. The principles above will apply to it identically.
</Callout>

## Cancellations

An order can be cancelled **at any stage before delivery**, by **the restaurant** or by **Grubano support** only. The customer cannot cancel it themselves from the app: they contact the restaurant or go through help. Once the order is **delivered**, it can no longer be cancelled.

Any cancellation triggers a **refund to the customer**, following the principles detailed above — returned commission included.

## Reservations: the card hold

To protect your table service against no-shows, [reservations](/en/guides/reservations/) can be secured with a **card hold**. It's an **available and optional** feature: you're the one who turns it on, by setting the no-show penalty amount. By default, this amount is **0 €** — the hold stays disabled until you set an amount.

<Steps>

### When the reservation is made

The customer's bank **authorizes** the hold amount. Nothing is charged: the customer pays nothing at this stage.

### When the customer arrives

The hold is **released**. An honored reservation costs the customer nothing, and the authorization disappears from their account.

### In case of a no-show

You can **capture the penalty**: the hold amount, the one you configured, is then charged.

</Steps>
