## The principle (and the why)

The affiliate program lets you recommend Grubano restaurants to your audience and earn a commission on every order generated. Unlike the [recipe creator](/en/guides/creators/) role, who publishes dishes adopted by restaurants, the affiliate shares a **referral link or code** and is paid on the **sales** they bring in, without creating any culinary content.

As soon as you sign up, a **personalized link** and a **unique code** are generated automatically: share them wherever you like (social media, blog, newsletter). Every order placed through your link is recorded, tracked and credited to your balance. Affiliates with a **verified audience** ([influencer](/en/guides/influencer/) status) enjoy extra benefits, but the underlying mechanism stays the same for everyone.

## Join the program and share your links

<Steps>

### Apply to the program

Head to the affiliate sign-up page (or use the "Become an affiliate" button if you're already logged in). Enter your name, email address and accept the terms. An affiliate account is created immediately: you receive a **unique referral code** (for example `ALEX9F2K`) and a **personalized link** (for example `/ref/alex9f2k`) shown on your dashboard.

### Get and share your links

Your affiliate dashboard shows your **referral link** and your **code**. You can copy the full link (which redirects to the Grubano home page) or build a direct link to a specific restaurant (the `/ref/<your-slug>` structure + restaurant parameter is generated automatically). Share these links on your social channels, in your content or by private message.

### Track your clicks and conversions

Every click on your link is recorded (anonymously, without any of the visitor's personal data). Your dashboard shows a **conversion funnel**: total number of clicks, number of attributed orders, conversion rate. This view helps you measure how effective your shares are.

### Check your earnings

Every order generated through your link appears in the "Earnings" tab with the **commission amount**, the **status** (pending or matured) and the date. **Pending** earnings become **matured** once the order is confirmed (after the cancellation window has passed); only matured earnings can be withdrawn. Your available balance is calculated automatically: matured earnings − amounts already paid out.

### Request a withdrawal

As soon as your balance reaches **25 €**, the "Withdraw" button becomes active on your dashboard. On your **first withdrawal**, you'll need to complete **identity verification** (Stripe Connect KYC, individual) and provide your **tax information** (address, tax number, date of birth) before the transfer is triggered. Subsequent withdrawals are automatic (the amount paid = your available balance, no manual entry). The transfer history appears below the withdraw button.

</Steps>

## A month at 300 € in commissions

Marie shares her Grubano affiliate link on Instagram and generates 15 orders over the month. Grubano takes a commission on each order (5 to 12% depending on the order mode); Marie earns a percentage of that commission, within the limits set by Grubano. By the end of the month, her dashboard shows **300 € in matured earnings**. She clicks "Withdraw": it's her first withdrawal, so she first completes Stripe identity verification (ID document) and fills in the tax form (address, French tax number). Once validated, the 300 € transfer is triggered to her bank account within a few days. Subsequent withdrawals will be instant (no new verification).

## Maximize your conversions

<Callout type="default">
- **Feature a specific restaurant** rather than the generic link: a `/ref/<your-slug>?restaurant=<id>` link points straight to the restaurant page and converts better than a link to the home page.
- **Mention your code** in your posts: some customers prefer to enter a code at checkout rather than click a link.
- **Track your stats** every week: the clicks → orders conversion rate tells you which content performs best.
- **Vary your formats**: story, post, short video — test what resonates with your audience.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Sharing a broken link**: always check that your link redirects correctly before publishing it widely.
- **Requesting a withdrawal too early**: as long as your balance is below 25 €, the button stays inactive; wait until you reach the threshold.
- **Neglecting identity verification**: on your first withdrawal, missing KYC blocks the transfer — have your ID document ready in advance.
- **Forgetting tax reporting**: even if identity verification is validated, the transfer won't go through without complete tax information.
</Callout>
