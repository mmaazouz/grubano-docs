## The principle (and the why)

The kitchen screen is reachable through the **Kitchen** entry in your [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/) menu, right below **Orders**. It filters your order flow to show only the **kitchen slice**: orders that are received, in progress or ready. Anything already picked up, delivered or cancelled leaves the screen automatically — the kitchen team only sees what's left to do.

The display **refreshes on its own every 8 seconds** as long as the screen is visible, and reloads immediately when you come back to the tab. Each ticket shows its mode — **Delivery**, **Click & collect** or **Dine-in** — with a dedicated icon, so you can plate and pack accordingly.

Kitchen and Orders work on the **same flow**: a ticket marked "Ready" on the kitchen side is reflected on the Orders page (and vice versa), each one seeing only the orders from its own restaurant.

## Moving a ticket forward

<Steps>

### Open the Kitchen screen

From the side menu of your dashboard, click **Kitchen**. The **Live** badge in the title confirms that the screen updates automatically — no manual reload needed.

### Read the summary bar

Three indicators at the top of the page: **Tickets in progress** (orders to prepare + in progress), **Average time in the kitchen**, and **Oldest ticket** — this last one turns to alert as soon as it has waited 20 minutes.

### Spot the urgent tickets

Each card shows the order's short number (its last 5 characters, in uppercase), the mode, the timer, then the item details: quantity, name, size, add-ons, exclusions ("no onions") and the customer's note. The color follows the real wait: normal under 10 minutes, caution between 10 and 19, alert at 20 minutes and beyond.

### Start the preparation

Tap **Start** on a ticket in the **To prepare** column: it switches to **In progress** right away — the change is immediate on screen, then confirmed by the server. If it fails (a network issue, for example), a "Couldn't update the ticket. Try again." message appears and the screen resyncs.

### Mark the dish ready

When the dish is plated, tap **Ready**: the ticket moves to the **Ready** column, with a "Ready" tag in place of the button. At this point, the kitchen's job is done: handing it over to the [customer](/en/guides/consumer/) or the courier is managed from the **Orders** page.

</Steps>

## In the thick of service

<Callout type="default">
- **Keep the screen displayed at all times**: the automatic refresh only runs when the tab is visible. If the screen was hidden, everything reloads the moment you return.
- **Handle the oldest ticket first**: the summary-bar indicator points it out at all times, and its switch to alert at 20 minutes is your safeguard against forgotten orders.
- **Tap "Start" at the real start of preparation**: the columns will faithfully reflect what's happening in the kitchen, both for you and for the front-of-house team.
- **Read the exclusions and notes before you start**: they're shown right on the ticket ("no…", the customer's note) — no need to open the order somewhere else.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Looking for a "Back" button**: the kitchen screen always moves forward. A ticket that has gone to "In progress" can't return to "To prepare" — the server refuses that kind of change.
- **Confusing "Ready" and "Handed over"**: marking a dish ready doesn't close the order. The ticket stays in the **Ready** column until it's handed over, which is tracked from the Orders page.
- **Worrying about payment collection**: no action on the kitchen screen touches payment — marking "Ready" charges nothing and credits no loyalty points (points are only sent once the order is delivered).
- **Running the kitchen from the Orders page alone**: possible, but less responsive — the Kitchen screen refreshes every 8 seconds (versus 15 for Orders) and shows only what concerns the kitchen team.
</Callout>
