## The principle (and the why)

Grubano is looking for independent couriers or delivery businesses to handle deliveries for its local restaurants. You apply with your SIREN or SIRET, specify your mission types (meals, supplier goods, B2B, refrigerated) and the vehicles you have available (bike, scooter, car, van, refrigerated). Once your business identity is validated automatically, you'll soon receive access to your dashboard.

Validation relies on a check against the official business register: Grubano confirms that your SIREN exists and that your entity is active. The contact name you declare may differ from your company's official name — it's simply a display name, never grounds for rejection. Once active, you'll be able to go available to receive mission offers, which you accept freely with no obligation. Your [earnings](/en/guides/driver-earnings/) are paid out for every delivery you complete.

## Apply and complete your first delivery

<Steps>

### Fill in the sign-up form

Go to the courier sign-up page. Enter your partner type (independent or business), your contact name, your email, your phone number and your SIREN (9 digits) or SIRET (14 digits). Specify the mission types you want to take on and the vehicles you have, along with your service areas.

### Automatic identity validation

Your SIREN or SIRET is verified automatically against the official business register. If your entity exists and is active, your application is approved even if the contact name you declared differs from the official name. If the entity can't be found or has been struck off, the application is rejected. Any technical issue (register unavailable, verification impossible) places your application in manual review.

### Wait on the waitlist

Courier account activation is currently on a waitlist. Your application has been recorded; you'll soon receive an email to access your workspace and start receiving missions. This waiting period lets Grubano roll out the logistics service gradually.

### Turn on your availability

Once your account is activated, log in to your courier workspace and switch the "Available" toggle to ON. You'll then receive mission offers matching your criteria (areas, mission types, vehicles).

### Browse the missions on offer

Missions matching your profile appear in the "Available missions" tab. Each mission shows the pickup restaurant, the delivery neighborhood (the exact address is only revealed once you accept the mission), the delivery type and your [earnings](/en/guides/driver-earnings/) amount.

### Accept a mission

Click "Accept" on the mission of your choice. If another courier accepted it in the meantime, you'll get a neutral notification ("Mission already assigned") with no penalty. Once accepted, the customer's full address is revealed to you.

### Pick up the order

Head to the indicated restaurant. Once there, mark "Order picked up" in the app. The mission moves to the next step.

### Deliver the order

Head to the customer's address. Once you've handed over the order, mark "Order delivered". The mission is complete and your earnings are recorded — the actual payout is coming soon (see the Earnings guide).

</Steps>

## Maximize your missions

<Callout type="default">
- **Turn on your availability** as soon as you're ready to receive missions — you'll only get offers if you're online.
- **Accept quickly** the missions you're interested in: other couriers see the same offers and can grab them first.
- **Mark each step** (pickup, delivery) in the app so the restaurant and the customer are kept informed in real time.
- **Turn missions down without worry**: if a mission doesn't suit you, don't accept it — no penalty and no rating will be applied to you.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Don't stay available if you're not ready**: turn off the "Available" toggle as soon as you take a break, otherwise you'll keep receiving offers.
- **Don't forget to mark "Picked up"**: until you confirm this step, the restaurant doesn't know you're on your way.
- **Don't accept a mission without checking** the delivery area: once accepted, you're committed to completing it.
</Callout>

## Prepare your application

<Callout type="info">
Before filling in the form, have your SIREN or SIRET number handy (it appears on your SIRENE registration certificate, which you can download for free from the INSEE website). If you're a sole trader (micro-entrepreneur), your SIRET is the same as your social security number followed by 5 digits. A valid, active SIREN or SIRET speeds up your automatic validation.
</Callout>
