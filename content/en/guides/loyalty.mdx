## The principle (and the why)

A returning customer costs less to acquire than a new one: the loyalty program turns a one-off purchase into a habit. Every euro spent automatically earns a point, visible in real time on the customer's [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/). The Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum tiers unlock without any manual action — the more the customer orders, the higher their status climbs and the greater the perks. The points balance becomes loyalty credit in euros, spendable on any order: the customer chooses when and how to use it, and you keep control of your margin.

## Enroll a customer in the program

<Steps>

### Collect the information

The customer provides their name, email, and optionally their phone number via the **POST /api/loyalty/register** route. A welcome bonus of 10 points is automatically credited at sign-up; the initial tier is Bronze.

### Check for uniqueness

The system detects duplicate emails: if an account already exists, the sign-up returns a 409 error with the message "An account already exists with this email." The customer immediately receives their unique referral code (the first 8 characters of the `referralCode`, in uppercase).

### View the wallet

The **GET /api/loyalty/wallet** route (authenticated, `restaurant` or `admin` role) displays the points balance, the current tier, the points needed to reach the next tier, and the last 10 validated orders. A signed-in customer can view their own wallet without the `?email=` parameter.

</Steps>

## Validate an order and credit points

<Steps>

### Record the order

After delivery, use **POST /api/loyalty/validate** with the customer's email, the order number (`uberOrderNumber`), the total amount, and the brand name. The system rounds the amount down to the nearest integer to calculate the points (example: 23,80 € = 23 points).

### Prevent duplicates

Each `uberOrderNumber` is unique: if the order has already been validated, the API returns a 409 error. The customer cannot earn the same points twice on a single order.

### Update the tier

The system automatically recalculates the tier based on the new balance: 0–99 pts = Bronze, 100–199 = Silver, 200–399 = Gold, 400+ = Platinum. The JSON response indicates the points earned, the new balance, the current tier, and the order identifier.

</Steps>

## The journey of a loyal customer

Marie joins the program and receives a **10-point** welcome bonus (Bronze tier). She orders **45 €** worth from your brand: 45 points are credited, and her balance rises to **55 points** (still Bronze). Two weeks later, she spends **72 €**: +72 points, balance **127 points** → she automatically moves up to **Silver**. After six orders over three months, she reaches **420 points** (Platinum tier) and decides to use **200 points** as loyalty credit on her next order; her balance drops back to **220 points** but she keeps her Gold status.

## Maximize engagement

<Callout type="default">

- **Communicate the balance** — show the number of points and the current tier on every order confirmation (email or notification).
- **Remind them of thresholds** — "Only 18 points to go to reach Silver!" encourages a quick reorder.
- **Highlight the loyalty credit** — express the balance in euros ("240 pts = 12 € in loyalty credit") to make the benefit tangible.
- **Encourage referrals** — the unique referral code (8 characters) lets the customer introduce your brand to friends and family; plan a bonus for both the referrer and the referee (the `referredBy` mechanism is present in the model).

</Callout>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">

- **Validating a cancelled order** — only credit points if the order was actually delivered; a later refund requires a manual balance adjustment.
- **Forgetting to fill in the brand** — the `brandName` field must exactly match the name recorded in the `Brand` table (a case-insensitive `contains` search, but a typo will block validation).
- **Double-crediting** — the uniqueness constraint on `uberOrderNumber` protects against duplicates server-side, but make sure your integration doesn't call the API twice in the event of a temporary network error.

</Callout>

## Automate point crediting

<Callout type="info">

Connect your partner platform's delivery webhook to **POST /api/loyalty/validate**: as soon as an order moves to the "delivered" status, the API automatically credits the points with no manual intervention. The customer sees their balance grow in real time, and you reduce the risk of oversights or delays.

</Callout>
