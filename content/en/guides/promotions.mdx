## The principle (and the why)

Each promotion is attached to a **brand** and describes a discount following one of the four types. The **percentage** applies a whole-number rate — between 1 and 90 — to the relevant cart. The **fixed amount** deducts a sum in euros, capped at the cart value — never a negative total. The **2nd item at −X%** discounts only the single cheapest unit in the relevant cart, and only from 2 eligible units onward. The **threshold** triggers when the subtotal reaches the level you set: the reward is either a percentage on the subtotal, or a **free item** (the cheapest unit among the dishes you selected).

You can refine each offer with optional conditions: a **cart minimum** (evaluated on the subtotal *before* discount), **targeted dishes** (the discount then applies only to those dishes on your [menu](/en/guides/menu/)) and the **modes concerned** (Delivery, Click & collect). A start/end **validity window** is required: outside the window, the offer does not apply.

On the customer side, everything is automatic: at the time of the order, the server resolves the brand's active promotions and applies **the most advantageous one** — with equal discounts, the oldest wins, to stay predictable. The customer never enters a code or an amount: the calculation is done 100% server-side. The promotion is also **mutually exclusive** with the [referral](/en/guides/consumer-loyalty/) discount (if a referral discount applies, the promotion is set aside) and with [loyalty](/en/guides/loyalty/) points, which combine neither with a promotion nor with a referral.

Finally, on the finance side: the [commission by mode](/en/guides/finances/) is calculated on the **amount paid** (subtotal − discount). The discount you grant therefore also reduces the commission charged — it is never yours alone to bear on the fee side.

## Create a promotion

<Steps>

### Open the Promotions page

From your [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/), open the **Promotions** page and select the relevant **brand**: each offer is attached to a specific brand.

### Name the offer and choose the type

Give it a name (at least 2 characters) and choose from the four available types: **Percentage**, **Fixed amount**, **2nd item** or **Threshold**.

### Set the value

For a percentage or a 2nd item: a whole number between **1 and 90**. For a fixed amount: a sum in euros **greater than 0**. For a threshold: a **threshold in euros greater than 0**, then the reward — a percentage (between 1 and 90) or a free item, with **at least one dish** in the list of free items (the form requires it, to protect your margin).

### Set the validity window

**Required start and end** dates and times, with the end strictly after the start. This is what lets you schedule time-limited offers.

### Fine-tune targeting if needed

Add a **cart minimum**, select **targeted dishes** from your own menu (a dish outside your menu is rejected) and restrict the offer to certain **modes** (Delivery, Click & collect).

### Activate, monitor, deactivate

The page distinguishes **automatic discounts** (percentage, fixed amount) from **offers** (2nd item, threshold). A promotion is **deactivated** with a single click but is never deleted: past orders reference it.

</Steps>

## Offers that pay off

<Callout type="default">
- **Clear your stock in one click**: the 'clear stock' shortcut directly creates a percentage promotion targeted at the relevant dishes, with a window running from now until the expiry date and a suggested −30% you can adjust. Handy alongside your [stock](/en/guides/stocks/) tracking.
- **Try the threshold with a free item** to push up the average cart: 'spend 25 €, get a free side' encourages customers to complete their order.
- **Use the validity window** for short flash offers rather than permanent discounts that eat into your margin.
- **Think about [creator](/en/guides/creators/) campaigns**: when you adopt a campaign dish, a percentage promotion tied to the campaign can be created right away, bounded by the campaign's dates.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Expecting stacking**: only one promotion applies per order — the best one. No point piling three active offers on the same brand; a note on the page reminds you.
- **Confusing the cart minimum with the amount after discount**: the minimum is evaluated on the subtotal *before* discount.
- **Creating a 'free item' threshold without choosing the dishes**: not possible — at least one free dish is required at creation.
- **Trying to delete a promotion**: you don't delete, you deactivate. A deactivated promotion immediately stops applying.
- **Assuming every offer is showcased on the customer side**: only percentage and fixed-amount promotions surface in the public storefront; 2nd item and threshold offers do apply to the cart, but without dedicated visibility.
</Callout>
