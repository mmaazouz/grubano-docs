## The principle (and the why)

The dashboard automatically aggregates every confirmed order during the day, whatever the mode (Delivery, Click & collect, Dine-in). It calculates total revenue, counts customers enrolled in the loyalty program, and identifies your best-performing brand.

Each metric refreshes as soon as a new order comes in, giving you an always up-to-date view without reloading the page. The change versus the previous day helps you anticipate: a sudden spike can signal an effective promotion or a local event, while a dip prompts you to check your [stock](/en/guides/stocks/) or restart a marketing campaign.

## Review your metrics for the day

<Steps>

### Open the dashboard

Log in to your restaurant account at `grubano.com/dashboard`. The home page immediately displays the four main metrics: today’s revenue, number of orders, loyalty customers, and top brand.

### Read the revenue

The first card shows the total amount collected since midnight. If you have multiple brands (multi-brand), this total aggregates them all.

### Identify your top brand

The "Top brand" card shows the name of the brand generating the most revenue today, along with its percentage of overall revenue. This metric helps you understand where to focus your efforts.

### Compare with yesterday

The change percentage (green if positive, red if negative) compares today’s revenue with yesterday’s. A large gap can reveal a shift in trend or a one-off event.

</Steps>

## Getting the most out of the dashboard

<Callout type="default">
- **Check it several times a day**: a glance at 11 a.m., 2 p.m., and 7 p.m. gives you three snapshots of the service and lets you anticipate the rushes.
- **Cross-check with [stock](/en/guides/stocks/)**: if a brand takes off, make sure you have enough ingredients to last the evening.
- **Note the events**: an unusual change (a match, a strike, the weather) is worth a mental note to adjust your future forecasts.
- **Compare across several days**: a single gap doesn’t make a trend — watch the whole week before changing your hours or your [menu](/en/guides/menu/).
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Ignoring alerts**: if the dashboard flags low stock or a delivery delay, clicking on it and handling the problem right away avoids stockouts in the middle of service.
- **Comparing days that aren’t comparable**: a Sunday vs a Monday, or a public holiday vs a working day, skews the analysis — prefer comparing week N with week N-1.
- **Ignoring the top brand**: if a brand underperforms over the long run, dig into the reasons (pricing, timing, reviews) instead of focusing only on the winner.
</Callout>
