## The principle (and the why)

Every customer payment — a delivery order, click & collect, table bill or reservation deposit — generates an **accounting entry** in your financial ledger, timestamped and tagged with the mode (dine-in, click & collect, delivery, reservation). The Grubano commission is deducted **at the source** based on the order's mode, before the money leaves for your bank account.

**Payouts** are automatic: as soon as a payment is confirmed by Stripe (a few seconds), the net amount is transferred to you within 2 to 7 business days, with no threshold and no manual action. You track the status in real time from your [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/).

## Track a payout

<Steps>

### Open the Finances page

From the side menu of your dashboard, click **Finances**. The default view shows your figures over a **rolling 7 days** (today + the previous 6 days).

### Read the summary at the top of the page

Four tiles give you the **gross collected** (the sum of all customer payments), the **Grubano commission** (the sum of the application fees deducted), the **net paid out** (what goes to your bank), and the **number of transactions** over the period.

### Change the period if needed

Three buttons let you switch between **Today** (since midnight), **Last 7 days** and **Last 30 days**. The totals recalculate instantly.

### Browse the transaction history

Further down, a table lists each **accounting entry**: type (`payment` for a collection, `refund` for a refund, `deposit_capture` for a captured reservation deposit), date and time, gross amount, commission, net, and the mode (dine-in, click & collect, delivery, reservation) when set. Rows are sorted from most recent to oldest.

### Identify the source of a transaction

Each row shows either a **table bill** (table name + status), a **reservation** (customer name + deposit status), or an **order** (click & collect or delivery, with payment status). You can see at a glance which sale generated the money.

### Spot the refunds

Rows of type `refund` appear as a **negative amount**: they represent the money returned to the customer. For each payment, the table also shows the **amount already refunded** (cumulative across all dates), so you can tell whether a bill is still partially refundable.

</Steps>

## Stay in control

<Callout type="default">
- **Check the Finances page every Monday** to confirm that the previous week's payouts have gone through.
- **Compare the modes**: if click & collect (8%) accounts for 60% of your revenue, your average commission will be far lower than 12%.
- **Reconcile with your bank statement**: transfers arrive within 2 to 7 days; a gap of a few days between the order date and the credit to your account is normal.
- **Keep an eye on the refund rate**: a figure above 5% may signal a quality or communication issue with customers.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Confusing gross and net**: the figure at the top of the page is the gross *before* commission; only budget against the net paid out.
- **Forgetting that Stripe fees are absorbed**: unlike other platforms, Grubano does not pass the banking fees back to you — the net shown is what you actually receive.
- **Looking for a minimum payout threshold**: there isn't one. Even 10 € is sent automatically to your bank.
- **Ignoring the order's mode**: a reservation generates *no* commission at all (0%); if you forget to enable it, you miss out on fee-free revenue.
</Callout>

## VAT & DAC7: your tax information

Your tax information is managed from the **Settings** page (the **More** entry on mobile), under **Legal & tax information**. Three blocks are relevant to you.

**Intra-EU VAT number**: enter your number (for example `FR 12 345 678 901`) then click **Save**. It will appear on the commission invoices Grubano sends you. Saving the field empty clears the number.

**DAC7 declaration**: a second form collects the information required by the European directive — seller type (company or individual), registered address, tax identification number (TIN) and its issuing country (two-letter ISO code); the date of birth is only requested if you declare as an individual. This information can be edited at any time, and it is the only DAC7 step expected on your side: Grubano handles the declaration to the tax authorities.

<Callout type="info">
The **business verification (KYB)** is shown in the same place, read-only: legal name, legal form, SIREN and verification status (**Up to date**, **In progress** or **Action required**, with the verification date where applicable). These items are checked by Grubano — you cannot edit them from this page.
</Callout>
