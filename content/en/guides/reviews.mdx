## The principle (and why)

Reviews come straight from your customers: from your restaurant's page in the app, a signed-in customer can rate their experience and write a comment — one review per customer per restaurant, which they can edit at any time (the new review replaces the old one). Only **published** reviews appear on your Reviews page, sorted from newest to oldest. This keeps your customer relationship yours: feedback lands with you, in the same place as your [orders and metrics](/en/guides/restaurant/).

To protect your customers' privacy, only the author's **first name** is shown next to their review. Each review also carries a **source** badge — "Grubano" today, the only review source on the platform. Your **average rating** is calculated from the breakdown by star (a weighted average from 1 to 5) and displayed with one decimal, along with the matching stars.

## Prepare a reply with AI

<Steps>

### Open the Reviews page

From your dashboard's side menu, click **Reviews**. The page automatically loads the reviews for the current restaurant (the one selected in your dashboard).

### Read the summary at the top of the page

The average rating, the breakdown of ratings from 1 to 5 stars, and the total number of reviews give you the overall temperature at a glance.

### Browse the reviews

The 50 most recent published reviews appear from newest to oldest: author's first name, rating, text, date, and source badge.

### Generate a reply draft

Open a review's reply window and click **Generate**. The AI writes a short suggested reply (fewer than three sentences), signed "The Grubano team," with a tone matched to the rating.

### Review and personalize

The draft appears in a fully editable text area: adjust it with your own words, a detail from the service, or a goodwill gesture. The **Send** button is still inactive — sending is coming soon; in the meantime, you can copy your text to reuse it wherever you like.

</Steps>

## Get the most out of AI drafts

<Callout type="default">
- **Let the AI adapt to the rating**: the draft's tone is chosen automatically — empathetic with an apology for a 1- or 2-star review, measured at 3 stars, enthusiastic from 4 upward.
- **Always personalize**: the draft is a starting point; a concrete detail (the dish ordered, the evening of the incident) makes the reply far more credible.
- **Watch the breakdown by star**: a rise in 1- or 2-star reviews shows up in the breakdown before it weighs on the average.
- **Keep it simple**: drafts are fewer than three sentences by design — a short, sincere reply beats a long plea.
</Callout>
