# Manage Your Network

Track the performance of your franchised outlets, verify accumulated royalties, and consolidate your reports — all from a single dashboard.

## The Essentials

When you become a franchisor on Grubano, you gain access to a dedicated dashboard that aggregates sales from all your franchised outlets, calculates royalties owed within the limits set by Grubano, and allows you to monitor your network’s health in real time.

## How It Works

Each outlet linked to your brand records its orders in the Grubano system. The franchisor dashboard automatically consolidates this data over a rolling 30-day window: revenue per outlet, number of orders, accumulated royalties. You can thus see at a glance which outlets are performing, how much you should receive, and where to focus your support. Financial terms (royalty rate, opening fees, exclusivity zones) are set between the parties concerned; the dashboard applies these parameters to calculate the amounts due.

## Step by Step

<Steps>

### Access the Dashboard

Log in with your franchisor account, then go to the **Franchise** → **Overview** space. There you will find the network summary: number of active outlets, cities covered, pending applications.

### Review Performance by Outlet

The "Outlets" section lists all franchised outlets: name, city, revenue over 30 days, number of orders. Outlets are ranked by revenue in descending order, which immediately highlights the network’s drivers and sites that need support.

### Verify Royalties

The "Royalties" block displays three amounts:  
— **Accumulated**: the total sum of royalties recorded since inception;  
— **Pending**: what has not yet been paid;  
— **Paid**: the history of payments already made.  
When the automatic deduction toggle is activated on the platform side, these lines are calculated order by order; otherwise, the amounts remain at zero and tracking is done manually.

### Explore Recent Activity

The timeline at the bottom of the page lists significant events: outlet openings, revenue milestones. It helps you spot trends (rapid growth in one area, slowdown elsewhere) and anticipate support needs.

</Steps>

## Best Practices

**Compare Performance During the Same Time Slot** — City-center restaurants and those in suburban areas do not have the same peak hours; adjust your expectations by taking into account each outlet’s profile.

**Identify Local Best Practices** — If an outlet displays an average basket significantly higher than the network average, analyze its menu or promotional strategy to replicate it elsewhere.

**Monitor Pending Applications** — An overly long processing time can discourage serious candidates; regularly check the application counter and process them within reasonable timeframes.

## Concrete Example

Imagine your brand has five outlets in Vaucluse. The dashboard displays consolidated revenue of €38,500 over the last thirty days, compared to €34,200 the previous period — a growth of +12.6%. You notice that Avignon alone generates €14,000, while Carpentras caps at €4,500. Digging deeper, you discover that Carpentras does not activate pickup on-site and closes on Sundays. You call the franchisee to suggest opening on Sunday lunchtime and activating pickup: two simple levers that, when deployed in Avignon, precisely boosted local revenue.
