## The principle (and the why)

Each brand has a centralized inventory: your ingredients, your containers, everything that goes into your production. For each item you set a **minimum acceptable quantity**; as soon as it is crossed, Grubano flags it on your [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/). The AI goes further: it observes the average consumption rate of each product and tells you how many days you have before you run out — enough time to order from your suppliers before you run dry.

Rather than entering each line by hand, you can also send a simple message in plain language ("I received 5 kg of chicken, there are 2 L of tomato sauce left, the rice is out"): the assistant parses your text, identifies the items and updates the quantities right away. A precious time-saver in the middle of a rush.

## Create and track a stock item

<Steps>

### Add a new item

From the Stocks page of your [menu](/en/guides/menu/), click **Add an item**. Enter the ingredient name (chicken, basmati rice, olive oil…), the initial quantity, the unit (kg, L, g, mL or u for unit/piece) and the minimum threshold that will trigger the alert.

### Set the restocking threshold

The **Minimum threshold** field is the level below which Grubano alerts you. For example, if you enter 2 kg for chicken, you will receive a notification as soon as the stock drops below this line.

### Get the alert and check the forecast

When an item crosses its threshold, a red dot appears in the stock list. The AI also computes the **number of days remaining before stockout** based on your recent average consumption — an indicator displayed under each product's name.

### Update quantities manually or in natural language

You can edit an item's quantity by clicking on it, or use the AI assistant: type "chicken 3 kg, tomato sauce out" in the dedicated field, and the AI updates the inventory for you in a second.

</Steps>

## What works well

<Callout type="default">
- **Realistic thresholds**: set the minimum threshold to a value that leaves you time to restock (2–3 days of consumption in general).
- **Daily updates**: refresh the quantities every morning or after each delivery so the forecasts stay reliable.
- **Expiration date filled in**: if an item has a short expiration date (fresh products), enter it; Grubano will remind you to use it up first.
- **Use the AI**: it saves you several minutes a day and reduces data-entry errors.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **Forgetting the threshold**: a threshold set to zero or missing never triggers an alert — you risk a stockout with no warning.
- **Ignoring the forecast**: the AI tells you "stockout in 2 days"? Order now, not tomorrow.
- **Inconsistent units**: if you alternate between "kg" and "g" for the same item, create two separate lines or standardize the unit to track thresholds correctly.
</Callout>
