# Inventory Management

Keep your inventory up to date in real time, detect stockouts before they impact your sales, and manage your restocking in natural language.

<FlowDiagram steps={["Add an item","Set a min threshold","Restock alert","7-day stockout forecast"]} />

## The Essentials

Inventory management allows you to monitor your quantities in real time, anticipate stockouts, and receive automatic alerts when a product falls below your restocking threshold. You can add or update your items manually, or simply send a message in natural language — the AI understands "chicken 3 kg, rice 5 kg, tomato sauce out" and updates the inventory for you.

## How It Works

Each brand has its own inventory: you list your ingredients, current quantities, and expiration dates. Grubano continuously monitors this data and alerts you as soon as a product reaches the critical threshold you have set. The goal: never run out without your customers knowing, and order at the right time.

AI-powered updates analyze your short messages ("2 kg of flour, tomatoes out") and automatically adjust the corresponding stocks. If the item already exists, its quantity is updated; otherwise, it is created. As a bonus, you get a **7-day stockout forecast**: for each product, Grubano estimates the number of days remaining based on average daily consumption and indicates the expected depletion date.

<Callout type="info">
The forecast relies on default averages (0.7 kg/day for kilograms, 5 units/day for pieces). Refine your thresholds over time to match your actual service pace.
</Callout>

## Step by Step

<Steps>

### Add an Item

From your [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/), open the **Inventory** page. Click **Add an item**, enter the ingredient name, current quantity, unit (kg, g, L, mL, or u for "unit"), then save. The item appears immediately in your inventory.

### Set a Restocking Threshold

Edit the item and specify the **minimum threshold** (for example, 2 kg). As soon as the quantity drops below this threshold, Grubano displays a "low stock" alert on the corresponding line. You can adjust this threshold at any time.

### Update in Natural Language

On the Inventory page, type a sentence like "chicken 3 kg, rice 5 kg, tomato sauce out" in the **Update via text** field. Confirm: the AI parses your message, recognizes the ingredients, updates the quantities (0 if "out"), and displays a summary of modified or created items. No item-by-item forms.

### Check the Forecasts

Once stocks are updated, consult the 7-day forecast table. Grubano indicates for each item the number of **days remaining**, the **estimated stockout date**, and a **status** (critical if ≤ 1 day, low if ≤ 3 days, ok otherwise). Order in advance items marked "critical" or "low."

### Track Expiration Dates

Enter an **expiration date** when creating or editing an item. Products near their expiration date appear at the top of the list to remind you to use them first or remove them from the [menu](/en/guides/menu/).

</Steps>

## Best Practices

- **Update after each delivery**: as soon as you receive a supplier package, add the new quantities. The forecast remains reliable only if your stocks are up to date.
- **Realistic thresholds**: a threshold that is too low causes stockouts; a threshold that is too high ties up cash. Observe your sales over a week and adjust gradually.
- **Use AI for quick updates**: at the end of service, dictate or type the day’s consumption in one sentence. It is faster than manually editing each line.
- **Link inventory and menu**: if a key ingredient is out, mark the corresponding dish as out of stock from the [Menu & AI Scan](/en/guides/menu/) page to avoid orders you cannot fulfill.

## Tips & Advice

Phrase your AI messages like a telegraphic list: "flour 5 kg, eggs 2 dozen, oil 3 L." The AI recognizes common synonyms ("dozen" → 12 u, "finished/out" → 0) and abbreviated units (kg, L). No need for complete sentences.

<Callout type="warning">
AI updates are subject to a monthly quota per restaurant. If you reach the limit, a 429 error message prompts you to wait until the following month or switch to manual entry.
</Callout>
