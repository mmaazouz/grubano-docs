# Becoming an Influencer

Transform your audience into affiliate revenue with verified status and restaurant partner programs.

<FlowDiagram steps={["Be an affiliate","Request verification","Join a program","Performance & earnings"]} />

## The essentials

An influencer is an [affiliate](/en/guides/affiliate/) whose audience has been verified by Grubano. This verified status grants you access to partner restaurant affiliate programs: you recommend their establishments to your community, and you earn a commission on the orders generated — within the limits set by Grubano. Your dashboard provides you with tools to analyze your performance and optimize your recommendations.

## How it works

Grubano distinguishes standard affiliates from verified influencers: when you have an engaged audience on a social platform (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok…), you can request audience verification. A Grubano administrator reviews your application — declared follower count, profile link, platform — and approves or denies the request.

Once verified, your profile displays the "Influencer" badge: you remain an affiliate, but you gain access to partner programs that restaurants offer specifically to creators with verified audiences. You join these programs, recommend the restaurants that interest you, and earn a commission on the orders you bring in. The exact amount of this commission is defined within the framework of the programs you join.

**Why this verification?** It protects restaurants: they invest in an advertising partnership and want to ensure the audience actually exists. It also protects you: an official status adds value to your work and facilitates collaboration negotiations.

<Callout type="info">
**Recipe creator ≠ influencer.** A creator publishes recipes that restaurants adopt and cook (they are paid for the use of their concepts). An influencer recommends existing restaurants via a referral link (they are paid on the orders brought in). You can combine both roles, but these are two distinct mechanisms.
</Callout>

### Be an active affiliate

You must first have a Grubano affiliate account. If you don’t yet have one, create your account: you instantly receive a personalized referral code and link. Consult the [affiliate documentation](/en/guides/affiliate/) to get started.

### Request audience verification

From your affiliate dashboard (`/affiliate/dashboard`), access the verification card. Fill in the requested information:

- **Main platform**: YouTube, Instagram, TikTok or other.
- **Public identifier** (handle): your @username on this platform.
- **Declared follower count**: the current number of your community.
- **Proof link** (optional): the URL of your public profile.

You can only submit one request at a time. If a request is already pending, you will not be able to create a new one until an administrator responds.

### Wait for administrative decision

A Grubano administrator reviews your application. They verify that your audience matches the declared profile. Three possible outcomes:

- **Approved**: your profile becomes "verified influencer." The badge appears on your dashboard.
- **Denied**: you receive a notification. You will be able to submit a new request after adjusting the information.
- **Pending**: the request is under review. No action is required from you.

### Join partner programs (coming soon)

Once verified, you gain access to the affiliate programs that restaurants open to influencers. You choose the establishments you wish to recommend, join their programs, and use your referral links to generate orders. The financial terms of each program — commission, duration, geographic scope — are set between the parties involved when you join the program.

## Best practices

**Authenticity first.** Recommend only restaurants you genuinely know and appreciate. Your community follows you for your sincerity: an overly visible commercial recommendation or one misaligned with your editorial line damages your credibility.

**Declare accurate numbers.** The declared follower count must correspond to your actual audience at the time of the request. An overestimation leads to denial and compromises your future requests.

**Track your performance.** Your dashboard shows you which restaurants convert best with your audience. Focus your efforts on partnerships that work, rather than multiplying scattered recommendations.

**Respect timelines.** Commissions are subject to a maturation period (legal withdrawal period). Your earnings become "acquired" after this delay; before that, they remain "pending." This is a protection for everyone: you, the customer and the restaurant.

## Common mistakes

<Callout type="warning">
**Requesting verification before being an affiliate.** Influencer status is an *additional layer* to the affiliate role, not an independent role. You must first create your affiliate account and obtain your referral link before you can request audience verification.
</Callout>

**Confusing audience verification and banking KYC.** Audience verification doesn’t involve money: it simply proves that you have a real community. Payment of earnings, however, will later require identity verification and registration (according to regulations in force). These are two separate processes.

**Submitting multiple simultaneous requests.** Only one request can be "pending" at a time. If you submit a new request while a previous one is awaiting a response, the system denies the new submission. Wait for the administrator’s decision before trying again.
