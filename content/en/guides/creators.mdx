# Recipe Creator

Submit your culinary creations to a network of local restaurants; adopted recipes earn you 4% of each sale, continuously.

<FlowDiagram steps={["Apply","Submit a recipe","Restaurant adoption","4% commission"]} />

## The Essentials

Are you a chef, cook, or cooking enthusiast? Publish your recipes on Grubano. When a partner restaurant adopts one of your creations and cooks it under their brand, you automatically receive 4% of the amount of each dish sold. Your commission runs as long as the dish remains on the restaurant’s [menu](/en/guides/menu/)—with no time limit or earnings cap.

## How It Works

Local restaurants are constantly looking for new concepts to enrich their offerings without dedicating R&D time. Your role: offer them ready-to-use recipes—name, description, ingredient list, cuisine type, photo, and suggested price. The restaurant selects those that match its positioning, adds them to its menu, and cooks them in its own kitchen. You retain intellectual ownership of the recipe; the restaurant benefits from a tested and validated dish, you from a passive income stream indexed to actual sales.

The platform automatically tracks each sale: as soon as an order contains a dish from your recipe, 4% of the sale price is credited to you. You view your earnings, the number of sales, and partner restaurants from your creator space, in real time.

## Best Practices

**Focus on visual clarity.** A clear, bright, mouth-watering photo multiplies your chances of adoption. Restaurants judge first on appearance—an appetizing dish sells more easily and justifies a premium price.

**Offer realistic recipes.** Favor common ingredients, techniques any restaurant cook can master, and reasonable preparation time. An overly complex recipe or one requiring rare equipment will be ignored; a simple and flavorful recipe will be adopted quickly.

**Play on variety.** Rather than five burger variations, diversify cuisine types (Mediterranean, Asian, vegetarian, comfort food) and formats (starter, main, dessert). You thus reach several restaurant segments and multiply your revenue sources.

**Set a consistent suggested price.** A price too low discourages adoption (the restaurant doubts the perceived quality); a price too high limits sales volume. Research prices locally practiced for the type of dish you propose.

## Common Mistakes

<Callout type="warning">
**Do not submit a recipe identical or nearly identical to a dish already present in the catalog.** Automatic verification detects conceptual duplicates and will reject your submission. Always bring an original touch—signature ingredient, particular cooking technique, distinctive presentation.
</Callout>

**Omitting allergen declaration.** If your recipe contains common allergens (gluten, lactose, nuts, shellfish, etc.), indicate them explicitly. If it contains none, select "No allergens" during submission. A missing declaration blocks publication.

**Neglecting the description.** A generic line ("A delicious dish") inspires no desire. Tell the dish’s story, detail the flavors, evoke the tasting experience. Restaurants seek creations they can showcase to their customers—help them by writing persuasive text.
