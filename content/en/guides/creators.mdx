## The principle (and the why)

You're passionate about cooking and you have ideas for original dishes. Rather than opening your own restaurant, you publish your recipes on Grubano. A partner restaurateur discovers them, adopts them, and cooks them in **their** kitchen, under **their** brand. You stay a creator, without managing stock or logistics.

On each order of your dish, you earn 2% of the sale price (excl. VAT). The restaurateur manages the [menu](/en/guides/menu/), the preparation, and the customer relationship; you track your earnings and the number of sales in real time.

## Publish a recipe and collect your earnings

<Steps>

### Apply to the program

Fill out the application form: name, email, bio, social media (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube), and up to 3 dish concepts (name, description, cuisine type). If you provide a YouTube link, paste the verification code you're given into your channel description to earn the verified badge.

### Submit a recipe

Once your profile is created, publish your detailed recipes: name, description, ingredients, cuisine type, suggested price, photo. You can save a draft (private) or submit directly. Submitted recipes go through an automatic check: approved, pending review, or rejected (with a reason).

### Restaurant adoption

Restaurateurs browse the catalog of approved recipes and adopt the ones that interest them. Once adopted, your recipe appears on the restaurant's menu, under its brand. You play no part in the preparation or the sale.

### Track your income

Every order of the dish generates a recorded sale. You earn 2% of the sale price (excl. VAT) per plate sold. The creator dashboard shows the number of sales per recipe, your total earnings, and the list of restaurants cooking your dishes.

</Steps>

## Maximize your chances of adoption

<Callout type="default">
- **An appetizing photo**: a professional image triples your chances of adoption.
- **A clear description**: precise ingredients, preparation steps, estimated time — the restaurateur needs to be able to reproduce the dish easily.
- **A consistent price**: a realistic suggested price (aligned with the cuisine type and the local market) reassures the restaurateur.
- **A complete profile**: bio, social media, verified badge if you have a YouTube audience — restaurateurs favor credible creators.
</Callout>

<Eyebrow icon="error">Mistakes to avoid</Eyebrow>

## Common pitfalls

<Callout type="error">
- **A recipe that's too complex**: a dish requiring 15 rare ingredients or 45 minutes of cooking will rarely be adopted; prioritize operational efficiency.
- **A lack of detail**: a vague description ("fresh salad") with no ingredient list will be rejected or ignored by restaurateurs.
- **An unrealistic price**: suggesting 25 € for a simple salad or 5 € for an elaborate dish undermines your credibility; align with the market.
- **Plagiarism**: submitting a recipe that already exists elsewhere (another creator, a well-known restaurant) results in an automatic rejection.
</Callout>
