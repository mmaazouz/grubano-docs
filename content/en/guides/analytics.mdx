## Two pages, two horizons

The **Analytics** page takes a step back: it aggregates your validated sales over the last 7 or 30 days and derives a trend chart, key metrics and a ranking of your brands. The figures are recalculated each time you open the page.

The **Briefing** looks at the day ahead: it gathers your figures for the day (validated revenue, orders, [reservations](/en/guides/reservations/), expected covers), spots the points that need attention ([low stock](/en/guides/stocks/), reservations to confirm, large groups) and hands it all to the AI, which writes a short summary displayed at the top of the page.

## Reading your analytics

<Steps>

### Open the Analytics page

From the side menu of your [dashboard](/en/guides/restaurant/), click **Analytics**. The default view covers the **last 30 days**.

### Read the key metrics

**Total revenue** follows the selected period (7 or 30 days). **Orders** and **Average order value** are calculated over 30 days only: in the 7-day view, they show “—” with the note “30-day only”. The **Covers** tile still carries a “Coming soon” tag, and the trend badges (comparison with the previous period) will arrive soon too.

### Change the period if needed

Two buttons toggle between **7 days** and **30 days**; the totals recalculate instantly. The **Custom** button (custom dates) is disabled for now (“Coming soon” tag).

### Track your sales trend

The chart traces your sales over the **last 7 days**, day by day, with the **7-day total** and the **Best day** calculated just above. The comparison with the previous week will be added soon — the graph says so honestly rather than showing a fictitious curve.

### Spot your peak hours

An intensity band shows your activity **hour by hour** (from 10 AM to 11 PM, across 6 intensity levels), calculated over the last 7 days. Your busiest hour is highlighted — “Peak at 7 PM”, for example. The cross day × hour breakdown will arrive soon.

### Check your brand ranking

The revenue and number of orders for each of your brands over **30 days**, sorted from highest to lowest revenue (top 5 shown). The ranking of best-selling dishes will arrive soon.

</Steps>

## Your morning check-in

Every morning, the **Briefing** page greets you by your first name and displays the day's real figures:

- **Validated revenue** and **orders for the day**;
- **Reservations for the day** and **expected covers** (sum of reserved covers);
- **Alerts**: [stock](/en/guides/stocks/) items close to their minimum threshold, reservations still to confirm, large groups (6 covers and up).

At the top of the page, the **AI summary** puts these figures into words: a short text written automatically from your data for the day (revenue, orders, reservations, covers, low stock) and displayed as-is. Further down, the **morning checklist** picks up your real alerts and adds routine checks (fridges, equipment): tick off each item, and the done/total counter follows your progress.

## Get into the rhythm

<Callout type="default">
- **Open the Briefing when you arrive in the morning**: stock alerts and reservations to confirm are best handled before service rather than in the middle of the rush.
- **Align your schedules with peak hours**: if the peak is at 7 PM, bring in extra kitchen staff from 6:30 PM.
- **Compare your brands over 30 days**: the ranking shows where to focus your efforts — and which [menu](/en/guides/menu/) deserves a refresh.
- **Switch to the 30-day view for the average order value**: it's the only period where this metric is calculated.
</Callout>
